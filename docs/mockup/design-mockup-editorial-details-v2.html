The Memorial

The National Katyń Memorial is a monument in Baltimore, Maryland, which memorializes the victims of the 1940 Katyń massacre of Polish nationals carried out by Soviet forces. Baltimore's Polish-American community was instrumental in having the monument built. The monument was unveiled in 2000 and is the tallest statue in Baltimore. The statue itself is 44 feet (13.4 m) high, the whole monument, with base, is 56 feet (17 m).

The statue by sculptor Andrzej Pityński was delivered from Poland and established in the Inner Harbor East at Aliceanna and President streets.

The Scan

The collection of 3D data for the National Katyń Memorial was performed by Direct Dimensions for the explicit purpose of conservation, restoration/reproduction and documentation at the behest of the National Katyń Memorial Foundation. Special consideration of scanned materials was needed to calculate surface areas of bronze and gold leaf individually in order to provide accurate details for preservation cost estimation. Details of the monument are restoration/replication grade, meaning that the data can be used to restore the monument to its scanned condition if further wear should occur.

Historical Context

The Katyń massacre was a series of mass executions of Poles carried out by the Soviet Union between April and May 1940. Nearly 22,000 Polish military and police officers, border guards, and intelligentsia prisoners of war were executed by the NKVD on Joseph Stalin's orders.

The massacre is qualified as a crime against humanity, a war crime and, according to a 2009 resolution of the Polish parliament, bears the hallmarks of a genocide.

Provenance

Funding for the monument was provided by the National Katyń Memorial Foundation and a grant provided by the state of Maryland.

Production took place over three years at the GZUT foundry in Gliwice, Poland, overseen by lead artist Andrzej Pityński. The unveiling occurred on November 19, 2000 at Katyń Circle, after a Mass at Holy Rosary Church.

Processing ▶
Software Reality Capture · Supersplat · Leica Cyclone · Brush

Photogrammetric capture from 483 photographs taken in circumferential passes at four different elevations around the statue as it is displayed onsite, outdoors in Baltimore, MD. Ambient mid-day daylight gave full even coverage. Lidar data also gathered at same time.

Bronze and gold leaf material surface area calculation was done by manually separating the main memorial model into two separate models based on material. Area was then calculated independently, resulting in an accurate surface area calculation for each material.
